About

I am an Assistant Professor of Social Anthropology at Mada Walabu University with over 10 years of experience in research, teaching, and community engagement. My research focuses on cultural studies, indigenous knowledge systems, GIS mapping, heritage tourism policy, and environmental/social impact assessments.

I hold a PhD from Addis Ababa University; my dissertation on the Great Dawuro Walls received recognition and has been presented at multiple international conferences. My academic journey includes a Postdoctoral Research Fellowship at the Transcultural Research Center, Erfurt University, Germany, where I researched "Beyond Cartography: Revisiting Antiono d'Abbadie's Studies in Making Enset Culture."

My professional experience spans various roles including Project Manager for the British Council's Cultural Protection Fund at Dessie Museum, qualitative researcher for National Democratic Institute, and environmental and social impact assessment consultant. I have published extensively on Dawuro heritage, defensive walls, and indigenous knowledge systems.

I'm committed to preserving cultural heritage while enabling community-driven development through research, policy recommendations, and practical interventions. My work has been featured on BBC Amharic, EBS TV, and various national media outlets, highlighting the importance of cultural preservation in modern Ethiopia.
