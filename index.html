About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of Social Anthropology at Mada Walabu University with over 10 years of experience in research, teaching, and community engagement. My work focuses on cultural studies, development policy, inclusion & marginalization, GIS mapping, indigenous knowledge, heritage tourism policy, and environmental/social impact assessment.

I hold a PhD in Social Anthropology from Addis Ababa University, where my research on "History, Heritage and Developmental Modernity in Dawuro: The Great Dawuro Walls/Kati Halala Keela as a Contested Site" received recognition. My work has been published in reputable journals and presented at international conferences.

Currently, I'm managing the British Council's Cultural Protection Fund project for Dessie Museum Renovation and continuing my research on indigenous knowledge systems and cultural heritage preservation in Ethiopia.
